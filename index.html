Bio

Omar I. Al-Bataineh is a Research Scientist at the Gran Sasso Science Institute in Italy. His research lies at the intersection of software engineering and formal methods, with particular focus on automated program repair, fault localization, and software verification. He received his Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Western Australia, and holds prior degrees from the University of New South Wales and Jordan University of Science and Technology. Before joining GSSI, he held research positions at Simula Research Laboratory, the National University of Singapore, and Nanyang Technological University.

Research

Al-Bataineh’s research focuses on advancing software engineering through formal methods, particularly in automated program repair, fault localization, and software verification. His work aims to bridge the gap between theoretical foundations of formal methods and their practical applications, especially for complex, multi-fault scenarios in software systems. He seeks to develop scalable, theoretically grounded methods for automating software repair, with an emphasis on improving the reliability and robustness of software systems.

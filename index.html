Bio

I am a Research Scientist at the Gran Sasso Science Institute (GSSI) in Italy. My research bridges formal methods and software engineering, with a focus on automated program repair (APR), fault localization, and software verification. I hold a Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Western Australia, with prior research positions at Simula Research Laboratory, the National University of Singapore, and Nanyang Technological University.

Research

I work on advancing automated program analysis and repair techniques. My research specializes in model checking, static analysis, runtime verification, and APR, aiming to bridge rigorous formal methods with practical software engineering challenges.

I focus on handling programs with multiple interacting faults and studying complex fault interactionsâ€”key barriers in reliable automated debugging. My goal is to enhance the effectiveness and scalability of APR tools for real-world applications.

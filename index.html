Bio

I am a Research Scientist at the Gran Sasso Science Institute in Italy. My research lies at the intersection of software engineering and formal methods, with a particular focus on automated program repair, fault localization, and software verification. I earned my Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Western Australia, and hold degrees from the University of New South Wales and Jordan University of Science and Technology. Before joining GSSI, I held research positions at Simula Research Laboratory, the National University of Singapore, and Nanyang Technological University.

Research

My research focuses on automated program analysis, program repair, model checking, static analysis, and fault localization, aiming to improve the reliability and scalability of software tools for real-world applications.

Theme 1: Multi-Fault Automated Program Repair

I study techniques for repairing programs affected by multiple interacting faults—bugs that manifest together within a single execution. My work focuses on designing lightweight and effective test oracles to guide patch generation, improving repair quality and reducing overfitting in complex fault scenarios.

Theme 2: Provably Reliable Program Repair

I explore the use of formal methods and decidable program fragments to generate patches that are not only correct but also provably reliable, advancing automated program repair toward high-assurance applications.

Theme 3: Termination-Aware and Context-Sensitive Repair

This research focuses on making automated repair practical by accounting for performance, termination behavior, and other context-dependent factors, aiming to produce patches that are both correct and efficient for real-world software systems.

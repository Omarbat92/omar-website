Research

My research spans formal methods and software engineering, with particular emphasis on model checking, static analysis, runtime verification, and automated program repair. The central objective of my work is to strengthen the integration between formal verification techniques and practical software development processes. Despite the significant potential of formal methods, their adoption in mainstream software engineering remains limited. My research aims to bridge this gap by developing scalable, theoretically grounded techniques that improve the reliability and robustness of software systems. A key focus is on advancing automated program repair to handle complex scenarios, including multi-fault programs, through principled approaches informed by formal reasoning.
